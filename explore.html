Calabar Carnival

Origin: Crossriver, Nigeria

Descrption: The largest street festival in Nigeria, celebrating culture, music, and dance with colorful parades.

Eyo Festival

Origin: Lagos, Nigeria

Descrption: The Eyo festival features masqueraders in white flowing robes and tall hats, parading through Lagos to honor the Oba and Yoruba tradition.

Durbar Festival

Origin: Northern, Nigeria

Descrption: A spectacular display of horsemanship, parades, and regalia celebrating Emirate traditions.

Argungu fishing Festival

Origin: Kebbi, Nigeria

Descrption: An annual fishing festival celebrating community, traditional fishing skills, and culture with lively music and dance.

Ofala Festival

Origin: Onitsha, Nigeria

Descrption: The Ofala festival celebrates the traditional ruler, featuring music, dance, and display of royal regalia.

Ojude Oba Festival

Origin: Ijebu-ode, Nigeria

Descrption: A colorful cultural festival celebrating the Oba, featuring horse riders, music, and traditional attire.

New Yam Festival

Origin: Anambara, Nigeria

Descrption: A harvest celebration marking the beginning of the yam season, symbolizing gratitude, renewal, and community among the Igbo people.

Sango Festival

Origin: Oyo, Nigeria

Descrption: A vibrant Yoruba celebration honoring Sango, the god of thunder and lightning, featuring drumming, dance, and fiery performances to invoke his power.

Egungun Masquerade Festival

Origin: Ekiti, Nigeria

Descrption: A vibrant celebration honoring ancestors and showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Yoruba people
